iACADEMY's History


iACADEMY was established in 2002 under the guidance of Eusebio H. Tanco, Washington Z. Sycip, and a group of board members recognized as experts in the fields of business and education. These distinguished men and women wanted to bridge a gap in the country’s educational system: the mismatch between graduates of academic institutions and the needs of the industry. To address the problem, iACADEMY’s founders envisioned a school that produces graduates that possess not just a diploma, but the skills and training that will prepare them well for the future.

Located along Ayala Avenue in Makati City, iACADEMY has grown since it opened its doors a decade ago. Its first course offerings included BSBA e-Management, BSCS Software Engineering, BSCS Network Engineering, and BSIT Digital Arts—courses designed to develop the technical and creative skills of its students.

For the succeeding school years, iACADEMY’s student population increased and its curriculum was enhanced. From the initial batch of 72 enrollees, the College now has nearly 1,000 students. In June 2003, the College teamed up with IBM Philippines for its computer science and information technology program. In 2005, the program was granted recognition, with zero deficiency, by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED).

To answer the changing imperatives of business and economy, new courses were offered like BS Entrepreneurship (2003), BSBA Marketing and Advertising (2004), BSBA Operations Management (2006), BS Fashion Design and Technology (2011), AB in Multimedia Arts and Design (2011). Plus, in line with its innovative roots, the iACADEMY was the first and remains the0 only school in the country to offer two cutting-edge courses: BS Animation (2007) and BS Game Development and Programming (2011).

iACADEMY has also forged strong bonds with respected entities in different industries. In 2009, WACOM, the world’s leading manufacturer of digital interface solutions as well as interactive pen displays and tablets became iACADEMY’s training partner. With this relationship, iACADEMY’s animation and multimedia programs have been tremendously enhanced. The College’s educational services are now at par with prestigious international colleges and universities. In 2010, iACADEMY was appointed as the first IBM Software Center of Excellence in the ASEAN Region and the first Lotus Academic Institute. As such, the College has proved to be one of the best training grounds for future IBM professionals, proof positive that iACADEMY’s curriculum is strong and competitive.

In 2011, just a few months after iACADEMY opened its BS Fashion Design and Technology program, it was chosen by Solar Entertainment Corporation to be the official partner-school and workspace of the Project Runway Philippines (Season 3), the popular reality TV show that has produced some of the country’s finest and fast-rising fashion designers.

iACADEMY Beyond
Pioneer of Innovation


Aside from quality instruction from iACADEMY faculty, the College boasts of state-of-the-art facilities, backed by the top-of-the-line equipment and software to help students maximize their schooling. Academic learning takes place mostly in classrooms, while computer laboratories provide students with simulations of workplace set-ups. Student meetings, general assemblies, and other related events and activities are held in conference rooms and other offices.

At iACADEMY, the possibilities are truly endless.

Mission


We transform our students into highly-valued members and significant contributors to society, as professionals and leaders in their chosen fields.

We build lasting and rewarding partnerships with leading industry players towards the development of professional and productive human capital.

We shall serve as a catalyst for the development and wide adoption of competency-based and industry-partnered education systems in the Philippines and other parts of the world.

Vision


iACADEMY will be a globally recognized leader in industry-aligned education, delivered through innovative, IT-enabled, and holistic programs.
